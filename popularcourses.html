Diploma In Computer Science

Duration

2 1/2 Years


Transfer Programme

– This diploma aims to give students transferable credits into various universities (local & overseas).

Curriculum & Delivery Style

- The curriculum is skill-driven & practical-oriented so that students can relate what they have learned to real-life examples and apply them in the workplace. Departing away from the conventional theory based approach, most of the subjects are delivered with an activity-based method where students learn by doing group activities in class, giving presentations, attending workshops by guest lecturers, and taking part in field trips, etc..


Programme Learning Outcome

i. Acquire knowledge of mathematics and computer science for the solution of complex IT problems.
ii. Describe the systematic approach to problem solving using a range of programming techniques and the common software applications.
iii. Demonstrate skill set in the development, testing and maintenance of software system practically.
iv. Work as part of a team using interpersonal skill to support team effort.
v. Communicate effectively in both oral and in written form with colleagues and team members.
vi. The ability to use IT to engage independent and life-long learning in work and studies for sourcing and processing information.
vii. Identify, formulate and analyse IT problems reaching substantiated conclusions using first principles of Mathematics.
viii. Demonstrate responsibility and apply this to one's own work, as a leader in a team, to manage projects in multidisciplinary environment.
ix. Demonstrate management principles and apply these to one's own work, as an entrepreneur, to manage projects in multidisciplinary environment.
x. Apply ethical principles and commit to professional ethics and responsibilities.

Diploma In Mathematics

Duration

3 Years


Transfer Programme

– This diploma aims to give students transferable credits into various universities (local & overseas).

Curriculum & Delivery Style

- The curriculum is skill-driven & practical-oriented so that students can relate what they have learned to real-life examples and apply them in the workplace. Departing away from the conventional theory based approach, most of the subjects are delivered with an activity-based method where students learn by doing group activities in class, giving presentations, attending workshops by guest lecturers, and taking part in field trips, etc..


Programme Learning Outcome

i. Graduates should be able to critically evaluate the strengths and weaknesses of study designs and can select a study design that is appropriate for addressing a specific research question.
ii. Graduates should be able to use statistical reasoning, formulate a problem in statistical terms, perform exploratory analysis of data by graphical and other means, and carry out a variety of formal inference procedures.
iii. Graduates should be able to describe important theoretical results and understand how they can be applied to answer statistical questions.
iv. Graduates should have familiarity with a standard statistical software packages and encourage study of data management and algorithmic problem solving.
v. Graduates should have strong communication skills which are necessary to effectively collaborate as part of interdisciplinary teams including the ability to interpret and communicate the results of a statistical analysis through oral and written reports.
